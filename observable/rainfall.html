Jakarta Rainfall
& Climate Patterns

Four years of satellite-derived daily precipitation, temperature, and humidity data for Jakarta, Indonesia — sourced directly from NASA's POWER API. 📍 6.2°S, 106.8°E — Jakarta 📅 2020 – 2023 🛰 NASA POWER / MERRA-2

About the Data Source

NASA's Prediction Of Worldwide Energy Resources (POWER) project provides surface meteorological and solar energy parameters derived from the MERRA-2 reanalysis model. The dataset covers the globe at 0.5° × 0.625° resolution and is updated with roughly a 4-day latency, making it one of the most accessible and reliable climate datasets for research and application development.

Why Jakarta?

Jakarta sits in a tropical monsoon climate (Köppen Aw), making it an ideal study site for precipitation dynamics. The city experiences two distinct seasons — a wet season from November to March driven by the northwest monsoon, and a drier period from May to September — with annual rainfall typically exceeding 1,800 mm. Understanding these patterns is critical for flood preparedness and urban planning.

Parameters Explained

PRECTOTCORR is the bias-corrected total precipitation (mm/day), adjusted against ground-based observations. T2M is 2-metre air temperature in °C, representing surface-level conditions. RH2M is relative humidity (%) at 2 metres — all three are daily aggregates from the MERRA-2 atmospheric reanalysis. Values of -999 indicate missing data and are excluded from calculations.
